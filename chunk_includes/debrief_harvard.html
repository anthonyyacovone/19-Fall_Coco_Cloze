DEBRIEFING:

What is this study about?

  • This study examines how easily people predict different words given previous sentence context.

  • People often have a difficult time predicting words at the beginning of a sentence.

Imagine predicting the word 'movies' given the sentences below:

1a. John has strong opinions about television shows but even more so about ___movies___
1b. John likes ___movies___

  • It is more difficult to accurately predict words with less context (1b).

  • With more context, people can better anticipate upcoming words (1a).

How is the study conducted?

In this task, you saw a story from beginning to end, sentence by sentence. You were asked to guess approximately 300 words and to read the parts of the story that came before and/or after. We are looking to see how predictions differ across words in these sentences.

A few more questions:

Did you think the story was interesting and engaging?


Overall, how predictable do you think the words were?
How well do you think you performed on this task?
